1. Market Overview

The US subscription video-on-demand market in 2021–2022 was characterised by three simultaneous dynamics: the continued dominance of established mega-platforms, rapid challenger growth fuelled by legacy broadcasters entering direct-to-consumer streaming, and pronounced fragmentation across 64 niche services. The aggregate subscriber pool grew from 372.6 million total quarterly subscriptions in Q1 2021 to 510.3 million by Q4 2022, a 37.0% increase over the full period. On a year-over-year basis (EOY 2021 to EOY 2022), the market expanded 15.3%, reflecting genuine subscriber growth and ongoing household multi-platform stacking.

3. Growth & Decline Analysis

While the aggregate market grew, performance diverged sharply at the platform level. On a year-over-year basis (Q4 2021 to Q4 2022), Fox Nation led all growth at +133.1% (516,679 → 1,204,178 subscribers), closely followed by Peacock Premium at +131.2% (9.1M → 21.0M). Screambox (+107.4%), Crunchyroll (+83.6%), Dropout (+75.7%) and BET+ (+74.6%) were also standout performers. Over the full 2021–2022 period, Peacock Premium grew cumulatively from 3.2M (Q1 2021) to 21.0M (Q4 2022), a total increase of +562.9%. Conversely, nine platforms recorded net subscriber losses over their tracked periods: BossTV (−21.2%), C Spire (−19.2%), WatchTV (−19.0%), WOW Presents Plus (−18.8%), Night Flight Plus (−10.0%), PBS Passport (−6.8%), PaulaDeen.com (−6.8%), AT&T TV Now (−3.6%) and Sling TV (−1.7%), reflecting competitive pressure from better-resourced rivals.

4. Market Segmentation

A segment-level view reveals a clear five-tier structure. Only two platforms exceed 50 million subscribers: Amazon Prime Video (98.8M) and Netflix (67.0M). Eleven platforms occupy the "Large" tier (10–50M): Hulu, Paramount+, Disney+, ESPN D2C, Apple TV+, HBO Max, Peacock Premium, YouTube Premium, Discovery+, Starz and Showtime Streaming. Eighteen platforms sit in the "Mid" tier (1–10M), 36 in "Small" (100K–1M), and 64 are "Niche" services with fewer than 100,000 subscribers each.

Strategic implication: The long tail of niche services faces existential viability pressure. With subscriber counts in the low tens of thousands, many platforms cannot achieve content investment and retention economics required to compete sustainably. Consolidation is the structural expectation for 2023 and beyond.

5. Netflix: The Flat-Top Giant

The most significant finding is Netflix's subscriber stagnation in the US market. After peaking at 67.8M in Q4 2021, Netflix contracted to 66.1M in Q2 2022 before recovering to 67.0M by year-end. This flatline contrasts sharply with year-over-year growth posted by Peacock Premium (+131.2%), Paramount+ (+47.8%) and Apple TV+ (+21.9%) over the same Q4 2021 to Q4 2022 window, confirming that Netflix had effectively saturated its addressable US audience by mid-2021.

Conclusion : US SVOD Market 2021–2022

The 2021–2022 period marks the end of the first wave of US streaming expansion. The market grew in aggregate, but dynamics shifted decisively: legacy media companies (Paramount+, Disney+) successfully established viable streaming presences, creating genuine multi-platform competition at scale. Amazon Prime Video consolidated its position as the largest US SVOD platform while Netflix navigated its first sustained growth plateau. The niche tier continues under existential pressure. The data reveals a market at structural inflection, moving from a growth phase to a competition-and-consolidation phase.
