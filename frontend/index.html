Research Highlights

Amazon & Netflix anchor a market that grew 37.0%, from 372.6M to 510.3M subscribers

The US SVOD aggregate subscriber pool grew from 372.6M in Q1 2021 to 510.3M by Q4 2022 (+37.0% full period; +15.3% YoY EOY 2021→2022), with Amazon Prime Video (98.8M) and Netflix (67.0M) as dominant anchors.

Source: Dataxis

Top 5 platforms hold 59.2% of all US SVOD subscriptions in Q4 2022

Amazon (98.8M), Netflix (67M), Hulu (48M), Paramount+ (46.3M) and Disney+ (41.9M) collectively dominate the landscape.

Fox Nation led YoY growth at +133.1%, Peacock Premium +131.2%, Screambox +107.4%

Top YoY growth leaders (Q4 2021 → Q4 2022): Fox Nation (+133.1%), Peacock Premium (+131.2%), Screambox (+107.4%), Crunchyroll (+83.6%), Dropout (+75.7%) and BET+ (+74.6%).

Netflix held flat at 67.0M while Peacock Premium grew +131.2% YoY, a market inflection point

Netflix peaked at 67.8M (Q4 2021) and recovered to only 67.0M by Q4 2022, while Peacock (+131.2%), Paramount+ (+47.8%) and Apple TV+ (+21.9%) each posted strong YoY gains.
